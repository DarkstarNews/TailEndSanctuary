About Us

Giving senior dogs a peaceful, loving ending to their lives.

Our Story

At Tail End Sanctuary, our work is simple at its core: to give senior dogs a peaceful, loving ending to their lives. This began with a pattern we could no longer ignore.

In our community, people kept identifying older stray dogs living on the streets on their own—dogs who were no longer young, no longer wanted, and often struggling just to get through each day. They were easy to overlook, but impossible to forget once you truly saw them.

What started as concern turned into action. One dog led to another, and it became clear that these senior dogs needed something different—something beyond traditional rescue.

What We Do

We provide a safe, stable environment for senior stray dogs in the Lake Chapala area of Mexico. Instead of leaving them in uncertainty we focus on comfort, care, and a sense of peace.

🍽️

Daily Care & Nourishment

Nutritious food and consistent daily routines.

🩺

Veterinary Support

Medical care and pain management tailored to each dog.

🏡

A Calm Environment

A secure, peaceful space where they can rest.

🤝

Human Companionship

Consistent presence and gentle, loving attention.

What Makes Us Different

We are not a traditional rescue. Our focus is on the quality of each dog's remaining time. For many of the dogs who come to us, this is the first time they experience consistent kindness.

Our Founders

Three people brought together by a shared concern for senior dogs.

👩

Jena Marie Olio

Co-Founder

Over 20 years in dog rescue and training, Jena runs a sanctuary for dogs whose owners have passed away. She has long been dedicated to caring for senior dogs in their final stage of life.

Ronni Coppola

Co-Founder

A lifelong dog lover who has rescued and fostered some of the most vulnerable dogs in the community. Her compassion and hands-on care make a daily difference in the lives of our dogs.

Juanita Crampton

Founder, Tail End Planning

Founder of the Tail End Planning program, helping pet owners prepare for their animals' future care. Her work led to a deeper focus on senior stray dogs and the creation of the sanctuary.
