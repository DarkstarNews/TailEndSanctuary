About Us

Giving senior dogs a peaceful, loving ending to their lives.

Our Story

At Tail End Sanctuary, our mission is simple: to give senior dogs a peaceful, loving ending to their lives.

This began with a pattern we could no longer ignore. In our community, people kept identifying older stray dogs living on the streets on their own—dogs who were no longer young, unwanted, and often struggling just to get through each day.

They were easy to overlook, but impossible to forget once you truly saw them.

What started as concern turned into action. One dog led to another, and it became clear that these senior dogs needed something different—something beyond traditional rescue.

What We Do

We provide a safe, stable environment for senior stray dogs in the Lake Chapala area of Mexico.

Instead of leaving them in uncertainty, we focus on comfort, care, and a sense of peace.

  • ✓ Daily care and nourishment
  • ✓ Veterinary support and pain management
  • ✓ A calm, secure environment
  • ✓ Consistent human companionship

What Makes Us Different

We are not a traditional rescue. Our focus is on the quality of each dog's remaining time. For many of the dogs who come to us this is the first time in their lives that they experience consistent kindness. The first time somebody notices they are in pain. The first time someone stays.

We measure success in peaceful days.

Our Founders

Three people brought together by a shared concern for senior dogs.

Jena Marie Olio

Over 20 years in dog rescue and training, Jena runs a sanctuary for dogs whose owners have passed away. She has long been dedicated to caring for senior dogs in their final stage of life.

Ronni Coppola

A lifelong dog lover who has rescued and fostered some of the most vulnerable dogs in the community. Her compassion and hands-on care make a daily difference in the lives of our dogs.

Juanita Crampton

Founder of the Tail End Planning program, helping pet owners prepare for their animals' future care. Her work led to a deeper focus on senior stray dogs and the creation of the sanctuary.

Be Part of This Work

Helping a dog doesn't always mean changing their whole life. Sometimes it just means making their final chapter peaceful and filled with love.
