About Us

The people and purpose behind Tail End Sanctuary.

Our Story

Tail End Sanctuary began with a pattern we could no longer ignore. In our community, people kept identifying older stray dogs living on the streets on their own—dogs who were no longer young, no longer wanted, and often struggling just to get through each day. They were easy to overlook, but impossible to forget once you truly saw them.

What started as concern turned into action. One dog led to another, and it became clear that these senior dogs needed something different—something beyond traditional rescue.

What We Do

Tail End Sanctuary provides a safe, stable environment for senior stray dogs in the Lake Chapala area of Mexico. We focus on dogs who are unlikely to be adopted due to age, health, or condition. Instead of placing them back into an uncertain future, we offer consistent care, comfort, and a peaceful place to live out their remaining time.

  • Daily care and nourishment
  • Veterinary support and pain management
  • A calm, secure environment
  • Consistent human companionship

What Makes Us Different

We are not a traditional rescue. Our focus is not on numbers or turnover, but on quality of life—ensuring that each dog is safe, comfortable, and cared for. For many of the dogs who come to us, this is the first time they experience consistent kindness.

How We Work

We are a small, hands-on sanctuary supported by caregivers, volunteers, and the community. Each dog receives individual attention based on their needs. We work with local veterinarians and rely on community support to continue providing care.

Transparency matters to us. Contributions go directly toward the daily care and medical needs of the dogs.

Our Founders

Jena Marie Olio

Over 20 years in dog rescue and training. Jena runs a sanctuary for dogs whose owners have passed away. She has long been dedicated to caring for senior dogs in their final stage of life.

Ronni Coppola

A lifelong dog lover and former dog show participant in the U.S., Ronni is known locally for rescuing and fostering the most vulnerable dogs in the community.

Juanita Crampton

Founder of the Tail End Planning program, helping pet owners prepare for their animals' future care. Her work led directly to the creation of the sanctuary.

Related Work

Tail End Sanctuary is connected to the Tail End Planning program. Tail End Planning supports dogs whose owners have made arrangements for their care. Tail End Sanctuary focuses on senior stray dogs who have no such support.
