About UsTail End Sanctuary

Our Story

Tail End Sanctuary began with a pattern we could no longer ignore.

In our community, people kept identifying older stray dogs living on the streets on their own—dogs who were no longer young, no longer wanted, and often struggling just to get through each day.

They were easy to overlook, but impossible to forget once you truly saw them.

What started as concern turned into action. One dog led to another, and it became clear that these senior dogs needed something different—something beyond traditional rescue.

What We Do

Tail End Sanctuary provides a safe, stable environment for senior stray dogs in the Lake Chapala area of Mexico.

We focus on dogs who are unlikely to be adopted due to age, health, or condition. Instead of placing them back into an uncertain future, we offer consistent care, comfort, and a peaceful place to live out their remaining time.

What Makes Us Different

We are not a traditional rescue.

Our focus is not on numbers or turnover, but on quality of life—ensuring that each dog is safe, comfortable, and cared for.

For many of the dogs who come to us, this is the first time they experience consistent kindness.

How We Work

We are a small, hands-on sanctuary supported by caregivers, volunteers, and the community. Each dog receives individual attention based on their needs.

We work with local veterinarians and rely on community support to continue providing care.

Transparency matters to us. Contributions go directly toward the daily care and medical needs of the dogs.

Our Founders

Tail End Sanctuary was created by three individuals brought together through a shared concern for senior dogs in the Lake Chapala community.

👩

Jena Marie Olio

Over 20 years in dog rescue and training, Jena runs a sanctuary for dogs whose owners have passed away. She has long been dedicated to caring for senior dogs in their final stage of life.

Ronni Coppola

A lifelong dog lover who has rescued and fostered some of the most vulnerable dogs in the community. Her compassion and hands-on care make a daily difference in the lives of our dogs.

Juanita Crampton

Founder of the Tail End Planning program, helping pet owners prepare for their animals' future care. Her work led to a deeper focus on senior stray dogs and the creation of the sanctuary.

Related Work

Tail End Sanctuary is connected to the Tail End Planning program.

Tail End Planning supports dogs whose owners have made arrangements for their care.

Tail End Sanctuary focuses on senior stray dogs who have no such support.

Though distinct, each serves a distinct purpose.
